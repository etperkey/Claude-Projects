"Where cash is king, the jukebox rules, and the Chicago Handshake flows freely"
Twelve months. Twelve legendary watering holes. From the neon-lit booths of L&L Tavern where serial killers once lurked, to the honky-tonk rhythms of Carol's Pub, this calendar celebrates the gritty, glorious dive bars that make Chicago the greatest drinking city in America. Grab a shot of Malort, order an Old Style, and raise a glass to the dives.

DID YOU KNOW?
Serial killers Jeffrey Dahmer & John Wayne Gacy were both regulars here. A hand-scrawled sign declares it "The Creepiest Bar in the USA."

L&L Tavern
3207 N Clark St, Lakeview
EST. 1934
Featured on Anthony Bourdain's "The Layover" and HBO's "Lovecraft Country." One of Chicago's best jukeboxes. Named after former owners "Lefty" and Lauretta.
Hours: 5PM-2AM (4AM Sat)
Must Try: Chicago Handshake
Vibe: Gloriously Grim
"The quintessential Chicago dive"

FEBRUARY 2026
Old Town Ale House
219 W North Ave, Old Town
EST. 1958
Roger Ebert called it "the best bar I know exists." Across from Second City, it hosted Belushi, Murray, and Bourdain. Cash only, open until 4AM.
Hours: 3PM-4AM Daily
Payment: Cash Only
Famous For: Political Nude Art
"A portrait gallery that serves booze"

DID YOU KNOW?
During Prohibition, owner Simon Lundberg ran a secret speakeasy here, likely getting whiskey from Al Capone. The drunk Viking fish sign = "pickled herring."

EST. 1934
"The Anchor of Andersonville." Mahogany bar modeled after a steamship, Viking ship decor, and famous secret-recipe glogg. Cozy fireplace in back.
Payment: Cash Only
Must Try: Glogg
Vibe: Swedish Time Capsule
"Feel like the richest people in the world"

Carol's Pub
4659 N Clark St, Uptown
EST. 1972
Chicago's only true honky-tonk, born during "Hillbilly Heaven" when 3.2 million Appalachian migrants moved here. Motto: "Drink More, Sing Better!"
Hours: Open until 4AM
Live Music: Thu-Sun
Famous For: Two-Stepping
"The last survivor of Chicago's country music history"

DID YOU KNOW?
Nelson Algren modeled "Tug and Maul" tavern after this bar. Liz Phair's "Exile in Guyville" cover was shot in the photo booth. Bartender Ken Ellis made 600+ quilts.

Rainbo Club
1150 N Damen Ave, Ukrainian Village
EST. ~1933
Started as burlesque and jazz hall, possibly a speakeasy. Featured in "High Fidelity." Red half-circle booths, iconic neon sign, and legendary photo booth.
Must Try: $2 PBR Drafts
Famous For: Photo Booth
Vibe: Indie/Alt-Rock History
"Best photo booth in the city"

DID YOU KNOW?
Goes through 31 gallons of Malort per week. Invented candy cane Malort in 2016. "Malortsgiving" and "Malortsmas" are annual traditions here.
JUNE 2026
Nisei Lounge
3439 N Sheffield Ave, Wrigleyville
EST. 1951
"Nisei" = second-generation Japanese immigrant. After WWII internment camps closed, displaced Japanese-Americans opened this bar. Last true dive in Wrigleyville.
Must Try: Infused Malort Flight
Games: Pool, Darts, 25+ Board Games
Food: BYOF Policy
"Fine purveyors of Malort since 1951"

DID YOU KNOW?
Openly defies Chicago's 2008 smoking ban. Paid thousands in fines, keeps a donation box for more. Strict "No Photos" policy. Victor Skrebneski published a whole book of photos here.
JULY 2026
Richard's Bar
491 N Milwaukee Ave, West Town
EST. 1925
At the legendary six-corner intersection that once divided North and South Side mobs. Tin ceiling, Rat Pack jukebox, hard-boiled eggs. Chicago's last smoker's bar.
Unique: Indoor Smoking
Snacks: Hard-Boiled Eggs (75c)
Deal: $2 Tecate Cans
"The smoking-friendly rebel with mob history"

Rossi's Liquors
412 N State St, River North
EST. 1974
Chicago's quintessential "slashie" - sit down for a shot, grab a six-pack for the road. Devoid of sunlight with permanent Christmas lights. Working-class oasis.
Type: Slashie (Bar + Store)
Payment: Cash Only
Location: Old Mob Dividing Line
"Grab a shot, grab a six-pack"

Big Chicks
5024 N Sheridan Rd, Uptown
EST. 1986
Owner Michelle Fire bought it during the AIDS epidemic and transformed it into an LGBTQ+ haven. "A place for everybody." One of world's largest private art photo collections.
Special: $1 Burgers (Mon)
Tradition: Free Sunday BBQ
Jukebox: Free (AIDS Charity)
"Where everyone belongs"

6036 N Broadway, Edgewater
EST. 1977
